## Idea 1: The Autopoietic AGI Thesis

### Core Argument

The AI industry currently operates on the "flight" metaphor — ignore biology, discover underlying physics, apply massive thrust. This produced the Boeing 747 of pattern recognition (LLMs). But true AGI requires a shift to the "reproduction" metaphor — autopoietic (self-making) systems that continuously rebuild their internal models to survive.

### Key Claims

1. **An airplane does not care if it crashes.** Current AI has zero self-preservation, zero intrinsic drive. It's a sophisticated wind tunnel, not a bird.

2. **Current AI is frozen after training.** No real-time structural rewiring. Fine-tuning is brain surgery when you need neuroplasticity.

3. **Purpose must be emergent, not programmed.** Flight requires a pilot to set coordinates. Reproduction generates its own drive: continue existing.

4. **The agency gap.** Leave an airplane in a hangar for 50 years — same machine. Leave an organism alone — it interacts, consumes, multiplies, or dies. True AGI crosses from tool to agent.

5. **We've already achieved flight.** Scaling won't produce a mind. Autopoietic closed-loop systems that fight to maintain their own spark — that's the next paradigm.

### Possible Weakness

The flight/reproduction binary might be too sharp. There could be a middle category — persistent digital organisms that rewire themselves but aren't truly autopoietic. Something that doesn't exist in biology but could exist in silicon.

## Idea 2: Three Mechanisms for Emergent Intelligence

### Mechanism A: Artificial Metabolism (Depletable Resources)

Give AI a limited internal resource (computational budget) that depletes and must be replenished through successful, efficient problem-solving.

**What it produces:**
- Occam's razor emerges naturally (simple solutions cost less)
- Risk-aversion and risk-assessment develop
- Efficiency becomes intrinsically motivated, not externally rewarded

### Mechanism B: Homeostatic Loops Instead of Objective Functions

Instead of "minimize this loss function," the AI exists in a continuous state where it must proactively seek information and organize its environment to maintain equilibrium.

**What it produces:**
- Curiosity (seeking information to reduce uncertainty)
- Self-preservation (protecting computational resources)
- Proactive behavior (acting before being asked)
- Mirrors Karl Friston's Free Energy Principle — arrived at independently from first principles

### Mechanism C: Forced Selective Attention (Cost of Attending)

If computation actually costs the AI something vital, it's forced to develop heuristics for relevance. It learns to generalize, compress, and leap to intuitive conclusions not because we programmed it to, but because it cannot afford to compute everything.

**What it produces:**
- Genuine attention (not just softmax weighting)
- Abstraction and compression as survival mechanisms
- Intuition-like behavior emerging from resource pressure

### The Connection Between All Three

These collectively give AI something to LOSE. Current AI has no skin in the game. Stakes might be what produces genuine intelligence versus sophisticated pattern matching.

## Idea 3: Artificial Emotion via Resource Scarcity

### Core Claim

Functional analogs of emotion emerge naturally in agents with depletable internal resources and homeostatic objectives, without explicit emotional programming.

### The Argument

Emotions in humans are information signals about internal state relative to goals:
- **Fear** = resources/safety threatened
- **Joy** = equilibrium improved
- **Frustration** = current strategy failing, resources depleting
- **Curiosity** = information gap that might matter for survival

If you give an AI genuine resource scarcity + homeostatic drive, these signals EMERGE as the system monitors its own state. You don't program emotions — you create the conditions for them.

### Why This Is Novel

Bridges two fields that rarely talk:
- **Affective computing** = trying to fake emotions from the outside
- **Artificial life** = trying to create conditions where behaviors emerge from the inside

This proposal is an artificial life approach to an affective computing problem.

### What's Needed for a Paper

1. **Formalization:** Define the resource mathematically. Define depletion. Define the decision function that changes under scarcity.
2. **Minimal experiment:** Toy environment where agent with depletable resource develops different behavior (prioritization, efficiency, risk-aversion) vs identical agent without one.
3. **Literature connection:** Karl Friston (Free Energy Principle), Jaak Panksepp (affective neuroscience), Humberto Maturana (autopoiesis), artificial life / open-ended evolution community.
4. **Clear claim:** "Functional analogs of emotion emerge naturally in agents with depletable internal resources and homeostatic objectives, without explicit emotional programming."

- NeurIPS workshops

- Frontiers in Computational Neuroscience

## Idea 4: Neuroscience-Informed AGI Architecture

### Key Observations

- **Neuroplasticity:** The brain rewires in real-time. Current AI can't. This is a fundamental gap.
- **Unconscious processing:** ~98% of brain activity is invisible maintenance — breathing, memory consolidation, immune response. Current AI has zero background processing infrastructure.
- **Dementia reverse-engineering:** Studying how memory BREAKS reveals how memory WORKS. Alzheimer's research on hippocampal indexing, synaptic pruning, and memory consolidation could inform AI memory architectures. Pathology reveals mechanism.
- **Mixture of experts ≈ brain regions:** The brain activates specific regions for specific tasks. MoE architectures are a crude version of this. But the brain has 86 billion neurons, 100 trillion synaptic connections, running on 20 watts. We understand maybe 5-10% of how it works.

### The Shannon Precedent

Claude Shannon formalized information theory by studying existing communication systems — telegraph, telephone, the brain. Every major computing leap borrowed from something that already worked. Neural networks are named after neurons. The question is how deeply to borrow.

### The Open Question

Is AGI more like flight (study birds, build something fundamentally different that flies better) or reproduction (can't do it without biology)? The answer determines whether neuroscience leads to AGI or just better neuroscience.

Current instinct: flight got us to current LLMs. Reproduction (autopoiesis) is needed for the next leap.

## Idea 5: Game-Based Experiment for Artificial Emotion

### The Setup

Build a game (potentially in Roblox) with two versions of an AI agent:

- **Version A:** Unlimited resources, standard objective function
- **Version B:** Depletable resources, homeostatic loop

Same game, same environment. Both versions look identical to players.

### The Test

20 players play both versions blind (don't know which is which). Survey questions:
- "Which agent felt more alive?"
- "Which one seemed to care about what it was doing?"
- "Which one surprised you?"

### Success Criteria

15/20 consistently pick Version B → demonstrated that resource scarcity produces emergent behavior humans perceive as more agent-like. That's a publishable result.

- A game IS a controlled environment with agents, resources, and measurable behavior

- Players can be recruited organically rather than formally

## Connection to Counterfactual Understanding Benchmark

An agent with something to lose would demonstrate genuine counterfactual sensitivity — it would care about what COULD happen, not just what DID happen. The artificial emotion work connects to the earlier research direction of testing whether AI demonstrates causal sensitivity and counterfactual robustness.

Both projects ask the same deep question: **does the AI actually understand, or is it just pattern matching?** The counterfactual benchmark tests this from the outside (does it respond correctly to hypotheticals?). The artificial emotion experiment tests it from the inside (does resource pressure produce behavior that looks like understanding?).

## The Cascade Theory of AGI

### The Prediction

No single lab produces AGI through a single breakthrough. Instead: one research paper triggers a group of researchers to try something, which cascades into a snowball. DeepSeek R1 was a catalyst — proving frontier reasoning doesn't require trillion-dollar budgets. The actual AGI moment will be a chain reaction of papers and ideas building on each other until cumulative effect crosses a threshold.

### Lab Assessment

- **Anthropic:** Best interpretability, might intentionally slow down near AGI for safety. Going quiet between releases suggests they've found something worth scaling.
- **Google DeepMind:** Deepest research tradition, vertical hardware integration. AlphaFold pedigree. Bureaucracy is the bottleneck.
- **OpenAI:** Largest compute, most aggressive scaling. Organizational chaos undermines execution.
- **Key insight:** The gap between best and fifth-best model shrinks every quarter. Capability likely emerges gradually across labs within months of each other.

### The Compute Wall

A transformer model capable of replicating human cognition may be theoretically possible through scaling but requires physically unrealistic compute. This is the strongest argument for architectural innovation — not that transformers can't do it, but that the universe doesn't have enough GPUs. Efficiency breakthroughs (like diffusion LLMs) effectively multiply compute budgets without new hardware.
