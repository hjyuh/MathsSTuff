## Definitions and a plain-English restatement of the theorem

### What `Set.IsAsymptoticAddBasisOfOrder o` means in this repository

### Plain-English restatement of `erdos_868.variants.Hartter_Nathanson`

Fix an integer `o > 1`. Then there exists a set `A ÔŐć ÔäĽ` such that:

- `A` is an asymptotic additive basis of order `o` (every sufficiently large natural number is a sum of `o` elements of `A`), and
- `A` contains **no minimal** asymptotic additive basis of order `o`:  
  whenever `B ÔŐć A` is itself an asymptotic additive basis of order `o`, there exists some `b Ôłł B` such that deleting `b` preserves the basis property, i.e. `B \ {b}` remains an asymptotic additive basis of order `o`.
